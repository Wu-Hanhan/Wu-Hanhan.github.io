I am a first-year PhD student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at [George Mason University](https://www.gmu.edu/), advised by [Prof. Weiwen Jiang](https://ece.gmu.edu/profiles/wjiang8). I received my B.E. in Computer Science and Technology from [Zhejiang University](https://www.zju.edu.cn/english/) in 2022.

## Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of **machine learning for sensing data**, **hardware-aware AI**, and **distributed scientific machine learning**. I am particularly interested in developing intelligent methods that are not only accurate but also practical under real-world constraints such as latency, energy, and communication efficiency.

Currently, I am working on projects that bridge quantum/classical sensing and deep learning, as well as hardware-physics co-guided frameworks for scientific machine learning. I contributed to EPIC, a hardware- and physics-co-guided distributed SciML framework for full-waveform inversion accepted at **DAC 2026**, which explores how edge-side encoding and physics-aware decoding can reduce communication latency and energy while preserving physical fidelity.

I am drawn to interdisciplinary problems that connect machine learning, physical insight, and practical system constraints. My long-term goal is to develop efficient AI systems that co-optimize across algorithms, hardware, and physical models to address important challenges in sensing, computing, and emerging technologies.
